About the Project

What is Diabetic Nephropathy?

Diabetic Nephropathy is a serious kidney complication of Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes, and a leading cause of Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) worldwide. Early detection can dramatically slow progression.

🎯

Objective

Build a machine-learning-powered tool that uses routine clinical and lab data to predict whether a patient is at risk of CKD due to diabetic nephropathy.

🧠

Model

An Artificial Neural Network (ANN) trained on the UCI Chronic Kidney Disease dataset. Inputs are scaled with a StandardScaler before inference to ensure consistent predictions.

📋

Inputs

The model accepts 14 numerical clinical measurements (e.g., hemoglobin, creatinine) and 10 categorical indicators (e.g., hypertension, diabetes mellitus) for a total of 24 features.

📊

Output

A binary classification — CKD Detected or No CKD — accompanied by a confidence score representing the model's probability estimate.

🔒

Access Control

The prediction module is secured behind a login system. Register an account to run predictions, ensuring patient data remains controlled and auditable.

⚡

Technology

Built with Python, Flask, TensorFlow/Keras, and scikit-learn. A lightweight yet powerful stack that brings clinical AI to the browser seamlessly.

Clinical Parameters Explained

All 24 parameters fed into the model — what they measure, their normal range, and their clinical significance.

# Parameter Type Normal Range / Values Clinical Significance
1 Age Numerical 1 – 120 years Risk of CKD increases significantly with age.
2 Blood Pressure Numerical ≤ 90 mm Hg (diastolic) Hypertension accelerates kidney damage.
3 Specific Gravity Numerical 1.005 – 1.030 Measures urine concentration; low values suggest impaired kidneys.
4 Albumin Numerical 0 – 5 scale Excess urine protein (proteinuria) is a hallmark of nephropathy.
5 Sugar Numerical 0 – 5 scale Glucose in urine (glucosuria) correlates with uncontrolled diabetes.
6 Blood Glucose Random Numerical < 140 mg/dL Elevated blood glucose damages glomerular capillaries over time.
7 Blood Urea Numerical 7 – 20 mg/dL High urea indicates the kidney's reduced ability to clear waste.
8 Serum Creatinine Numerical 0.6 – 1.2 mg/dL Primary marker of kidney filtration function (GFR surrogate).
9 Sodium Numerical 135 – 145 mEq/L Electrolyte imbalances reflect kidney regulation failure.
10 Potassium Numerical 3.5 – 5.0 mEq/L Hyperkalemia is a dangerous complication of CKD.
11 Hemoglobin Numerical 13.5 – 17.5 g/dL (M) Anemia is common in CKD due to reduced erythropoietin production.
12 Packed Cell Volume Numerical 38 – 54 % Hematocrit; low values confirm CKD-related anemia.
13 White Blood Cell Count Numerical 4,000 – 11,000 cells/cumm Elevated WBC may indicate concurrent infection or inflammation.
14 Red Blood Cell Count Numerical 4.5 – 5.9 million/cumm Reduced RBC reflects renal anaemia common in kidney disease.
15 RBC Condition Categorical Normal / Abnormal Dysmorphic red cells in urine suggest glomerular disease.
16 Pus Cell Categorical Normal / Abnormal Increased pus cells (pyuria) indicate urinary tract infection or inflammation.
17 Pus Cell Clumps Categorical Present / Not Present Clumping suggests significant kidney or urinary tract infection.
18 Bacteria Categorical Present / Not Present Bacteriuria can trigger or exacerbate kidney damage in diabetics.
19 Hypertension Categorical Yes / No A major independent risk factor that doubles the rate of CKD progression.
20 Diabetes Mellitus Categorical Yes / No The root cause of diabetic nephropathy; leads to glomerulosclerosis.
21 Coronary Artery Disease Categorical Yes / No Shared vascular risk with CKD; indicates systemic endothelial dysfunction.
22 Appetite Categorical Good / Poor Poor appetite is a symptom of uraemia in advanced kidney disease.
23 Pedal Edema Categorical Yes / No Fluid retention due to protein loss and reduced kidney function.
24 Anemia Categorical Yes / No Impaired erythropoietin secretion in failing kidneys leads to anaemia.
